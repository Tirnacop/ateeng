1920 The Jazz Era

The status of the woman has evolved for good, which is also reflected in clothing, attitude, makeup, appearances in society. Thus, the 1920's fashion model focused on a new style, a bolder and a colourful one. 

Plus sized Fashion

.

The plus-sized clothing was inspired by the free-spirited women of the 1920s. Amazing beaded or sequin dresses and long flowing art deco inspired gowns drape from the body in effortless comfort 
Most 1920s plus size flapper dresses had sleeves and a midi or long length too. 

1930 Glamouros Era

 If we look at classic films from the 1930s and 1940s, we can't only see but also feel that life was more glamorous  

Back then, movie stars were always movie stars, even in everyday life. When photographed, they played tennis in white clothes, perfectly arranged, or on fabulous nights, wearing evening dresses, furs and diamonds as beautiful tasting martini.  
When it comes to makeup, blush. The desired effect in the 1930s was not harsh, wholesome or outdoorsy, so bright pink cheeks were not in vogue. A light application of cream powder (apricot for day, rose for a more bright look) can be compared with what we use today
The brow of the 1930s is thin. As was true in the 1920s, the brow is plucked into a single, thin line. In some cases, the brow is shaved off, then contoured in pencil.
Eyeliner pencil was a must-have in the 1930s. Often, the entire upper and lower lid were filled in pencil.  

1940 Pin-up Era

The year was dominated by Britain's involvement in the Second World War. Although the war continued, Britain won in the Battle of Britain and stopped Nazi Germany's invasion attempt

Pin up Style

.

The vintage inspired pin up dresses with A-line, pencil or circle skirts that moves with your steps was an amazing detail. A wide cinch belt helps define the waist and add a contrasting color to your pinup fashion. The strapless pin up dresses are the most popular 40s pinup clothing for summertime.

Dresses

.

1940 is unique by silhouette for its simple shapes and strong ties to history. Lifestyle was hard in the ’40s, but not without nice details! Simple shirtwaist dresses were the daytime classic, wrap dresses added variety, and afternoon looks like evening dresses mixed with velvet columns. We found many great new dresses all featuring elements of 1940s fashion. 40s tea dresses, WW2 era dresses, 40s dance dresses, vintage dresses, retro dresses and more.

Accessories

.

The purse and hat were the two biggest 1940s accessories a woman wore. Most women wore a belt on each piece of clothing. They were necessary but not emphasized until the late 1940s. Gloves had more style being worn whenever a woman went outside the houses. They were usually worn with a purse or hat.
